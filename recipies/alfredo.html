Fettucine Alfredo

A smooth, rich cheese sauce over al dente fettucine.

Fettucine Alfredo is thought to have originated in Rome in the early 20th cenutry. The name may have evolved from "Fettucine alla Alfredo", or "Fettucine in the style of Alfredo", with the name referring to Alfredo Di Lelio, a Roman restauranteur credited with the dish's invention. Over time, Fettuncine Alfredo has become a staple of cusine on an international level, with subtle differences across the globe.

The Italian combination of pasta, butter, and cheese dates back to at least the 1400s. According to Di Lelio family history, Alfredo began working at a restaurant in Piazza Rosa run by his mother. It was there that he cooked his first fettucine al triplo burro, or fettucine with triple butter, which later became known as fettucine alfredo. While the original recipe includes only three ingredients, fettucine, young Parmesean cheese, and butter, there are legends about a secret original ingredient. Chefs disagree about what this ingredient might be, or whether it exists at all.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tsp garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 2 cups fresh grated parmesean

Steps

  1. Add the butter and cream to a large skillet
  2. Simmer over low heat for 2 minutes
  3. Whisk in the garlic, Italian seasoning, salt and pepper for 1 minute
  5. Whisk in the parmesan cheese until melted
  6. Serve immediately
